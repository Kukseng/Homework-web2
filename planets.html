Planets of the Solar System

Discover the eight unique worlds orbiting our Sun, each with its own story.

Start Exploring

Our solar system consists of eight planets orbiting around the Sun. Each planet has unique characteristics that make it special. Explore the fascinating worlds that share our cosmic neighborhood!

Planet Size Comparison

Relative size comparison of the eight planets (not to scale)

Mercury

The smallest and innermost planet, Mercury is a rocky world with a heavily cratered surface similar to the Moon's appearance.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 36 million miles
  • Diameter: 3,031 miles
  • Day length: 59 Earth days
  • Year length: 88 Earth days

Did you know? Despite being the closest planet to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest planet. Venus holds that distinction due to its thick atmosphere.

Venus

Often called Earth's sister planet due to similar size, Venus has a toxic atmosphere and extreme greenhouse effect making it the hottest planet.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 67 million miles
  • Diameter: 7,521 miles
  • Day length: 243 Earth days
  • Year length: 225 Earth days

Did you know? Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets, meaning the sun rises in the west and sets in the east.

Earth

Our home planet, known for its diverse ecosystems and liquid water. Earth is the only known planet to support life.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 93 million miles
  • Diameter: 7,926 miles
  • Day length: 24 hours
  • Year length: 365.25 days

Did you know? 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, giving it the nickname "The Blue Planet."

Mars

The red planet, a prime target for future human exploration. Mars has polar ice caps, valleys, and the largest volcano in the solar system.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 142 million miles
  • Diameter: 4,212 miles
  • Day length: 24.6 hours
  • Year length: 687 Earth days

Did you know? Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the tallest mountain in the solar system at 72,000 feet high.

Jupiter

The largest planet, famous for its Great Red Spot and many moons. Jupiter is a gas giant with no solid surface.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 484 million miles
  • Diameter: 86,881 miles
  • Day length: 10 hours
  • Year length: 12 Earth years

Did you know? The Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging for at least 400 years and is large enough to fit 3 Earths inside.

Saturn

Best known for its stunning ring system, Saturn is a gas giant with a complex and beautiful appearance.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 886 million miles
  • Diameter: 72,367 miles
  • Day length: 10.7 hours
  • Year length: 29.5 Earth years

Did you know? Saturn is so light that it would float in water if there were an ocean big enough to hold it.

Uranus

An ice giant with a distinctive blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. Uranus rotates on its side, unlike other planets.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 1.8 billion miles
  • Diameter: 31,518 miles
  • Day length: 17 hours
  • Year length: 84 Earth years

Did you know? Uranus rotates at a 98-degree angle, making it appear to spin on its side as it orbits the Sun.

Neptune

The windiest planet in our solar system, Neptune is a distant ice giant with a deep blue color and powerful storms.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 2.8 billion miles
  • Diameter: 30,599 miles
  • Day length: 16 hours
  • Year length: 165 Earth years

Did you know? Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of over 1,200 mph.
